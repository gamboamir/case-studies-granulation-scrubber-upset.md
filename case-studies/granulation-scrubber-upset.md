Urea Granulation Scrubber Upset – Field Troubleshooting Case Study
Process Area

Urea Granulation Unit – First Bed Cooler & Scrubber System

Situation

During normal plant operation, the level in the first bed cooler increased excessively, which caused a carryover of solid urea particles into the granulator scrubber instead of only dust particles.

This resulted in a high urea concentration inside the scrubber solution, leading to operational instability.

Problem Observed

Several abnormal conditions were detected:

Scrubber solution concentration increased significantly.

Urea solids began accumulating in the system.

Scrubber circulation pump tripped.

Standby pump also tripped shortly after start-up.

Scrubber level continued increasing.

Open drain line became clogged due to crystallized urea.

This created a risk of scrubber overflow and process upset.

Immediate Actions Taken

The tripped circulation pump was isolated and depressurized.

Mechanical team was requested to open and clean the pump suction strainer.

Standby pump was attempted but also tripped due to blockage.

Communication was maintained with DCS operators and shift supervisor.

Field Diagnosis

Inspection and observation suggested that:

High concentration of urea caused crystallization and solid deposition in:

Pump casing

Suction line

Drain line

This blockage prevented normal circulation of scrubber solution.

Corrective Action Implemented

To dissolve the deposited urea, a decision was made to inject Low Pressure Water (LW) into the system.

Injection conditions:

Pressure: 9 kg/cm²

Temperature: ~120°C

Water was injected through:

Pump suction line

Pump casing

Recirculation line back to the scrubber

Result

The hot water injection caused:

Increase in scrubber solution temperature

Dissolution of crystallized urea deposits

Opening of the previously clogged drain line

Restoration of proper circulation

After the blockage was cleared:

The pump was successfully restarted.

Scrubber level stabilized.

Normal operation resumed.

Meanwhile, plant management addressed the root cause at the first bed cooler.

Key Lessons

This event highlighted several important operational insights:

High urea concentration can quickly cause crystallization and equipment blockage.

Maintaining scrubber solution temperature is critical for preventing solid deposition.

Field troubleshooting requires coordination between:

Field operator

DCS operator

Mechanical team

Shift supervisor.

Operator Competencies Demonstrated

Process troubleshooting

Equipment isolation and depressurization

Field decision making

Communication with DCS

Understanding of urea crystallization behavior
