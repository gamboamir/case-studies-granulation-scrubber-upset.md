Granulation Unit Case Study
Scrubber Pump Trip Caused by High Urea Concentration
Unit
Urea Granulation Unit
Equipment Involved
Granulator
Safety Screens
First Bed Cooler
Granulator Scrubber
Scrubber Circulation Pumps
Pump Strainer
Scrubber Drain System
Situation Overview
During normal granulation operation, the level in the First Bed Cooler increased significantly.
This caused excessive carryover of urea particles toward the granulator scrubber.
Instead of only dust particles reaching the scrubber, large quantities of urea material entered the scrubber system, increasing the urea concentration in the scrubber solution.
Initial Problem
The high concentration caused:
Scrubber circulation pump tripping
Standby pump also tripping
Strainer blockage due to urea solids
Rapid increase of scrubber level
The situation created a risk of:
Scrubber overflow
Loss of scrubbing efficiency
Potential plant upset
Immediate Actions Taken
Pump Isolation
The primary pump was isolated.
Pump was depressurized safely.
Mechanical Intervention
Mechanical team opened the pump strainer.
Strainer was removed and sent for cleaning.
Standby Pump Attempt
Standby pump was started but also tripped due to blockage.
Drain Attempt
DCS instructed opening the scrubber drain to reduce level.
However, the drain became clogged due to high urea concentration.
Root Cause Observation
The high concentration of urea in the scrubber solution caused:
Crystallization and solid deposition
Blockage in:
Pump suction
Pump casing
Drain line
This prevented normal circulation and drainage.
Field Solution Implemented
To dissolve the accumulated urea solids, I decided to inject Low Pressure Water (LW).
LW Conditions
Pressure: 9 kg/cm²
Temperature: ~120°C
Injection points:
Pump suction line
Pump casing
Recirculation line
Flow path back to scrubber
Result of the Action
The hot water injection caused:
Dissolution of solid urea deposits
Increase in scrubber solution temperature
Gradual clearing of pump internal blockage
Scrubber drain reopened naturally
Restoration of normal circulation
After confirming conditions with DCS, the scrubber pump was restarted successfully.
Parallel Operation
While resolving the scrubber issue:
The shift manager focused on stabilizing the first bed cooler level.
Mechanical team completed strainer cleaning.
Final Outcome
The scrubber system was stabilized by:
Increasing solution temperature
Dissolving urea solids
Restoring pump circulation
Normal operation resumed.
Key Operational Lessons
High urea concentration can cause rapid crystallization and blockage.
Scrubber systems require temperature control to avoid solid deposition.
Hot water injection can be an effective field solution to dissolve urea solids.
Coordination between field operators, DCS, and mechanical teams is critical during upset conditions.
Operator Competencies Demonstrated
Process upset diagnosis
Field troubleshooting
Equipment isolation and safety compliance
Process chemistry understanding (urea solubility)
Communication with DCS and maintenance teams
Why This Case Study Is Valuable
This incident highlights the importance of quick field analysis and process understanding when dealing with crystallizing materials such as urea.
